abstract: We present high-resolution hydrodynamic and MHD simulations of a Perseus-like cool-core cluster with self-regulated AGN feedback and Lagrangian tracer particles. By following the thermodynamic and dynamical histories of gas elements in an unbiased, volume-filling way, we characterize the conditions leading to condensation and assess the role of magnetic fields throughout the full cycle of cold gas formation and evolution. In both runs, the tracer population transitioning from the hot to the cold phase is dominated by hot gas entrained onto cold, low-entropy clumps rather than by in-situ radiative cooling. In the MHD run, the condensing population begins to diverge kinematically from the non-condensing background as early as 150 Myr before the cooling transition, compared to only 30 Myr in the purely hydrodynamical run. Magnetic tension is found to be the dominant drag force on infalling ballistic clouds, counteracting up to 100% of the gravitational acceleration in the final 20–30 kpc of infall.

# Summary. An optional shortened abstract.
summary: We use Lagrangian tracer particles in hydrodynamic and MHD simulations of a Perseus-like cool-core cluster to characterize the thermodynamic pathways leading to cold gas condensation, finding that mixing dominates over in-situ cooling and that magnetic fields substantially impact the assembly and kinematics of the cold phase.
